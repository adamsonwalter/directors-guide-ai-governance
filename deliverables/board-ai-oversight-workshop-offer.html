A live, facilitated board session anchored to the AICD/HTI Director's Guide to AI Governance (v2, June 2026) and a corrected, audited knowledge bundle. What's included

Live diagnostic session

2 hours, in a boardroom on a shared screen or over video conference. Each of the board's actual tools and decision flows is assessed live against the five-condition applicability test — not a generic checklist.

Turnover-scaled exposure

Penalty figures calculated against the organisation's own turnover, current from 1 July 2026 — infringement notices, civil penalties, and the s13G serious-interference ceiling.

Board-ready report

ADM Action Register, red-flag register from the Operating Model walk, statutory-tort and s180 exposure summary, and a resolution set ready for board minutes — delivered within five business days.

Session structure

  • Open and baseline — entity profile, compliance clock (15 min)
  • Operating Model walk — strategy, structure, practices, enablers (25 min)
  • Privacy Spearhead — live diagnostic, tool-by-tool (55 min)
  • Facilitator Q&A — grounded, live (15 min)
  • Close — action register and resolutions (10 min)

Who it's for

Boards and executive teams of Australian companies and not-for-profits that use, or are evaluating, AI systems, rules engines, or decision-driving software that touches customer, employee, applicant, or member data. Particularly relevant where a board cannot yet answer: which tools would trigger the disclosure duty, and who is accountable for finding out.

Pre-session ask

A one-page list of systems, spreadsheets, or vendor products touching a decision about a person — requested five business days out, from the company secretary or CFO.
