# Board AI Oversight Workshop — Facilitator Run-of-Show (2 hours)

Companion to `PROJECT_INSTRUCTIONS.md` and the `board-workshop-diagnostic` Cowork artifact.
Two delivery modes are supported without changing the structure — differences are called out
inline.

**In-room:** one screen at the front, Walter facilitates from a laptop mirrored to it, board
follows the artifact as tools are assessed live.
**Video conference:** Walter shares the artifact tab; the Cowork chat runs in a second window
for sync/report generation, visible only to Walter.

Pre-session (send 3–5 business days out): a one-page tool/decision-flow pre-read request — ask
the company secretary or CFO to list every system, spreadsheet, or vendor product that touches
a decision about a customer, employee, applicant, or member. This seeds Segment 2 so the room
isn't starting the inventory cold.

---

## Segment 1 — Open and baseline (15 min)

- Frame the session: not a legal opinion, a working session that leaves the board with an
  honest picture of exposure and a board-ready action register.
- Confirm entity profile in the artifact header: organisation name, annual turnover, listed
  status — this drives the penalty-ladder scaling for the rest of the session.
- State the compliance clock once, plainly: OAIC binding guidance on the unsettled drafting
  points is expected ~September 2026; the disclosure duty commences 10 December 2026 regardless.
  The session's job is to have the inventory and draft-ready structure in place before that
  guidance lands, not to finalise wording today.

## Segment 2 — Operating Model walk (25 min)

Paraphrased director questions and red flags — never reproduce the Guide's own lists verbatim;
this is a live conversation, not a read-out.

- **Strategy** (~6 min): Can the board state its AI strategy in one paragraph? Is there a risk
  appetite for AI, differentiated by use case?
- **Structure** (~6 min): Does the board know where AI and decision-driving software is used?
  Is one named senior person accountable?
- **Practices** (~8 min): What gives the board confidence it meets its legal obligations? Does
  the risk process distinguish high-risk from low-risk applications? — this segment hands off
  directly into Segment 3.
- **Enablers** (~5 min): Has anyone assessed stakeholder impact? Does the board know its data
  quality?

Capture red-flag hits verbally; they feed the resolution set at the close.

## Segment 3 — Privacy Spearhead: live diagnostic (55 min — the core of the session)

Open the `board-workshop-diagnostic` artifact on the shared screen.

1. **Penalty ladder orientation (5 min).** Point at the turnover-scaled figures from Segment 1's
   entity profile. Keep the tiers separate: a deficient privacy policy is typically **s13K**
   (1,000 PU = A$364,000 per contravention for a body corporate); **s13H** and **s13G** are
   broader interference / serious-or-repeated ceilings — do **not** treat the s13G 30%-of-turnover
   figure as the automatic price of a privacy-policy miss (see HE-4 / CORPUS worked case at
   A$14M). Use the ladder as risk framing, not a single conflated maximum.
2. **Statutory tort and s180 briefing (10 min).** Read the two flag panels aloud. This is where
   "we didn't know" stops being a safe answer — ASIC Report 798 makes the absence of an
   inventory itself evidence of the s180 gap, independent of whether any individual tool later
   turns out to trigger the disclosure duty. Also state the board-use boundary once: AI used to
   digest board papers is not, by itself, an APP 1.7 trigger.
3. **Tool-by-tool walkthrough (35 min, ~4–5 min per tool, 6–8 tools typical for a 2-hour
   session).** For each tool from the pre-read: enter name and description, step through the
   five-condition APP 1.7 test live (artifact `ADM_STEPS` — never substitute the Guide's p.39
   "explain when ADM systems are used…" summary), let the room answer each YES/NO (don't answer
   for them — the board's own uncertainty on a question is itself useful signal), add the outcome
   to the ledger. Flag statutory-tort exposure separately for anything biometric,
   facial-recognition, or covert-monitoring in nature.
4. **Mid-session sync (5 min).** Click "Copy session ledger" in the artifact. Paste into the
   Cowork chat (second window on video conference; brief pause on in-room) and say "sync
   ledger". Confirm the running register updates before continuing — this is the manual step
   that makes the artifact's state durable beyond the browser tab.

## Segment 4 — Facilitator Q&A (15 min)

Open the floor. Use the artifact's Q&A panel for quick factual lookups grounded in the session
corpus (exemption boundaries, penalty figures, the human-sign-off point) so the room sees the
answer, not just hears it. Anything genuinely novel or fact-specific gets parked for the report
and a counsel referral, not answered live.

## Segment 5 — Close: action register and resolutions (10 min)

- Final "sync ledger" click and paste.
- Read back the cumulative ledger stats (tools assessed / disclosure triggered / tort flags)
  as the headline number the board takes into its minutes.
- State next steps in one paragraph: who owns the register, when it's reviewed again (recommend
  before the OAIC's ~September 2026 guidance lands, and again before 10 December 2026), and that
  policy drafting language itself waits for that guidance rather than being finalised today.
- Confirm delivery of the written report within [X] business days (see commercial offer for the
  committed turnaround).

1. Pull the final synced ledger from the Cowork chat.
2. Generate the board report: ADM Action Register (one row per tool, condition-that-decided,
   confidence, disclosure-trigger flag), red-flag register from Segment 2, statutory-tort/s180
   exposure summary, and a resolution set ready for board minutes.
3. Register any new organisational facts worth retaining permanently (not just this session's
   ledger) through the OKF bundle's own `AGENTS.MD` orchestrator, per `PROJECT_INSTRUCTIONS.md`.
4. Deliver the report per the committed turnaround in the commercial offer.
